Cuisines of India

Indian cuisine is incredibly diverse, with regional variations influenced by local ingredients, culture, and history. Each region offers unique flavors and cooking techniques.

Regional Culinary Highlights:

  • North Indian Cuisine: Known for rich gravies, tandoori breads, and dairy. Popular in Punjab, Uttar Pradesh, Delhi. Famous dishes include Butter Chicken, Naan, Biryani.
  • South Indian Cuisine: Characterized by rice-based dishes, coconut, and spices. Popular in Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka. Dishes include Dosa, Idli, Sambar, Hyderabadi Biryani (Telangana).
  • East Indian Cuisine: Often features fish, mustard oil, and subtle spices. Popular in West Bengal, Odisha. Famous dishes include Macher Jhol, Rosogolla.
  • West Indian Cuisine: Diverse, ranging from sweet flavors of Gujarat to spicy Maharashtrian dishes. Popular in Gujarat, Maharashtra, Goa. Dishes include Dhokla, Vada Pav, Goan Fish Curry.
  • Andhra Pradesh Cuisine: Renowned for its spicy and tangy flavors, use of tamarind, and chili. Famous dishes include Pesarattu, Gongura Pachadi, spicy curries.
