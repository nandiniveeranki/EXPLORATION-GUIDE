Ports in India

India has a long coastline dotted with major and minor ports, crucial for its trade, economy, and maritime security. These ports handle a vast volume of cargo and play a significant role in international trade.

Major Ports & Their Significance:

  • East Coast Ports:
    • Visakhapatnam Port (Andhra Pradesh): One of India's oldest and deepest ports, handling various cargo including iron ore, petroleum, and fertilizers.
    • Chennai Port (Tamil Nadu): An artificial harbor, one of the oldest in India, known for containers, automobiles, and general cargo.
    • Paradip Port (Odisha): A deep-water port, primarily handling iron ore, coal, and other bulk cargo.
    • Kolkata Port (West Bengal): An inland riverine port, handling diverse cargo including tea, jute, and coal.
  • West Coast Ports:
    • Mumbai Port / Jawaharlal Nehru Port (JNPT) (Maharashtra): JNPT is India's largest container port, near Mumbai. Mumbai Port handles general cargo.
    • Kandla Port (Gujarat): A tidal port, one of the busiest, especially for petroleum, chemicals, and iron & steel.
    • Kochi Port (Kerala): A natural harbor, important for containers, petroleum products, and spices.
    • Mangaluru Port (Karnataka): Handles commodities like iron ore, fertilizers, and petroleum.
