Forestry in India

India is blessed with diverse forest ecosystems, including tropical evergreen, deciduous, mangrove, and coniferous forests, providing vital ecological services and biodiversity.

Key Forested Regions & National Parks:

  • Western Ghats: Rich biodiversity, evergreen forests (Kerala, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Goa, Maharashtra). Examples: Periyar National Park, Silent Valley National Park.
  • Eastern Himalayas: Unique flora and fauna (Sikkim, Arunachal Pradesh, Assam). Example: Kaziranga National Park (Assam).
  • Central Highlands: Deciduous forests, tiger reserves (Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh). Examples: Bandhavgarh National Park, Kanha National Park.
  • Sundarbans: Largest mangrove forest in the world (West Bengal, Bangladesh).
  • Andhra Pradesh Forests: Nallamala Hills (part of Eastern Ghats - Kurnool, Prakasam districts), Seshachalam Hills (Chittoor, Kadapa districts), Papikondalu National Park (East Godavari, West Godavari).
