Mines & Minerals of India

India has a rich geological heritage, endowed with a vast repository of mineral resources that have been the bedrock of its industrial and economic development for centuries.

Coal & Lignite

Often called "black gold," coal is the backbone of India's energy sector, fueling a majority of its thermal power plants. India is one of the top producers globally, with vast reserves concentrated in states like Jharkhand, Odisha, Chhattisgarh, and West Bengal. The industry, spearheaded by Coal India Limited, is critical for the nation's energy security and industrial manufacturing.

Petroleum & Natural Gas

While India is a net importer, domestic production of petroleum and natural gas is vital. Major production centers are located offshore in the Mumbai High basin and onshore in states like Assam and Gujarat. Organisations like ONGC and Oil India Ltd. are at the forefront of exploration and production, striving to enhance India's self-reliance in the hydrocarbon sector.

Iron Ore

India is endowed with high-quality iron ore reserves, primarily hematite and magnetite, which form the foundation of its robust iron and steel industry. The mineral is extensively mined in the states of Odisha, Karnataka, and Jharkhand. The country is a leading global producer and exporter, supplying a critical raw material for infrastructure development worldwide.

Bauxite

Bauxite is the primary ore for aluminium, a versatile metal crucial for industries like transportation, construction, and packaging. India holds significant bauxite reserves, with Odisha being the largest producing state, accounting for over half of the country's output. The mining of bauxite is integral to companies like NALCO and Hindalco, making India a key player in the global aluminium market.

Copper

Copper's excellent electrical conductivity makes it indispensable for the power and electronics industries. India's primary copper reserves are found in the Malanjkhand belt in Madhya Pradesh and the Khetri belt in Rajasthan. While domestic production does not meet the entire demand, mining operations by companies like Hindustan Copper Ltd. are vital for the nation's industrial self-sufficiency.

Diamonds

India has a historic legacy with diamonds, with the famed Golconda mines being the source of legendary gems like the Koh-i-Noor. Today, the only active diamond mine in India is the Panna mine in Madhya Pradesh. While production is limited, India is the world's largest cutting and polishing hub for diamonds, with Surat in Gujarat processing the vast majority of the world's rough diamonds.

Gold

Gold holds immense cultural and economic significance in India. Historically, the Kolar Gold Fields (KGF) in Karnataka were a major source. Currently, the Hutti Gold Mine in Karnataka is the primary producer of gold in the country. Despite modest domestic production, India is one of the largest consumers of gold in the world, driven by its use in jewellery and as an investment.

Mica

India is the world's largest producer of sheet mica, an essential mineral known for its perfect cleavage and insulating properties. It is indispensable in the electrical and electronics industries. The primary reserves are located in the Koderma belt in Jharkhand and in parts of Andhra Pradesh and Rajasthan. India's dominance in the mica industry is a testament to its unique geological resources.

Limestone

Limestone is a crucial raw material for the cement industry, and is also used in steel manufacturing and chemical production. India has extensive deposits of high-grade limestone distributed across states like Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, Andhra Pradesh, and Gujarat. Its abundance has fueled the growth of India's massive cement industry, which is vital for infrastructure development.
