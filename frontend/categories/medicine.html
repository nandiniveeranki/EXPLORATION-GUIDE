Medicine in India

India has a dual healthcare system, with a long history of traditional medical practices like Ayurveda and Yoga, alongside a rapidly developing modern allopathic medical infrastructure.

Key Aspects & Centers:

  • Traditional Medicine:
    • Ayurveda: Originating in ancient India, practiced widely (e.g., Kerala known for Ayurvedic resorts and treatment centers).
    • Yoga & Naturopathy: Popular across India, with many centers in Rishikesh, Mysuru.
    • Unani, Siddha, and Homoeopathy: Other prominent traditional systems.
  • Modern Healthcare:
    • Major Medical Hubs: Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Pune, Ahmedabad, Vellore (Christian Medical College).
    • Research & Education: AIIMS (All India Institute of Medical Sciences) in Delhi and other cities, numerous medical colleges and research institutions.
    • Medical Tourism: India is a growing destination for medical tourism due to quality care at lower costs.
  • Pharmaceutical Industry: India is a major global producer of generic medicines and vaccines, with manufacturing hubs in Gujarat, Maharashtra, Andhra Pradesh, and Telangana.
