Finance in India

India's financial landscape is a dynamic blend of traditional institutions and cutting-edge technology, powering one of the world's fastest-growing major economies.

Reserve Bank of India (RBI)

The **RBI** is India's central bank and the supreme monetary authority. Established in 1935, it is responsible for issuing currency, framing monetary policy to control inflation, managing the country's foreign exchange reserves, and regulating the entire banking sector to ensure financial stability and public confidence.

Public Sector Banks (PSBs)

PSBs are banks where a majority stake is held by the government. Led by the **State Bank of India (SBI)**, they have been the pillars of the Indian economy, driving financial inclusion with their vast branch network across the country. They play a crucial role in providing credit to key sectors like agriculture and small-scale industries.

Private Sector Banks

Private sector banks like **HDFC Bank** and **ICICI Bank** have been at the forefront of innovation in the Indian banking industry. Known for their customer-centric approach and rapid adoption of technology, they have pioneered digital banking services, from mobile apps to internet banking, setting new benchmarks for efficiency and convenience.

Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE)

Established in 1875, the **BSE** is Asia's oldest stock exchange. Located at Dalal Street in Mumbai, it has played a pivotal role in developing India's capital markets. Its benchmark index, the **S&P BSE SENSEX**, is a widely tracked indicator of the health of the Indian economy. The BSE has a market capitalization of trillions of US dollars.

National Stock Exchange (NSE)

Incorporated in 1992, the **NSE** was a pioneer in introducing a modern, fully-automated, screen-based electronic trading system. It is India's largest financial market and a global leader in derivatives trading. Its flagship index, the **NIFTY 50**, represents the 50 largest and most liquid Indian securities, serving as a barometer for the Indian capital market.

SEBI

The **Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI)** is the statutory regulatory body for the securities and commodity market in India. Its primary functions are to protect the interests of investors, promote the development of the securities market, and regulate its activities to ensure fair and transparent dealings.

Unified Payments Interface (UPI)

**UPI** is a revolutionary real-time payment system that has transformed digital transactions in India. Developed by NPCI, it allows for instant money transfers between bank accounts using a mobile device. Its simplicity and interoperability have led to exponential growth, making it a cornerstone of India's FinTech ecosystem and a model for the world.

Digital Wallets & Payments

Beyond UPI, India's FinTech landscape is bustling with digital wallets and payment gateways like Paytm, PhonePe, and Google Pay. These platforms have accelerated the shift away from cash by offering a wide range of services, including mobile recharges, bill payments, and merchant transactions, further deepening digital financial inclusion.

1991 Economic Reforms

The economic reforms of 1991 are a watershed moment in India's financial history. The policies of **LPG (Liberalisation, Privatisation, and Globalisation)** opened up the Indian economy to the world. They delicensed industries, reduced trade barriers, and encouraged foreign investment, setting the stage for decades of high economic growth.

Goods & Services Tax (GST)

Introduced in 2017, the **GST** is the most significant tax reform in independent India. It subsumed a multitude of central and state taxes into a single, unified tax structure. The "One Nation, One Tax" system has simplified the indirect tax regime, improved compliance, and created a common national market, enhancing the ease of doing business.
