Architecture in India

India's architectural landscape is a testament to its diverse history, religions, and empires, ranging from ancient to colonial and modern styles.

Notable Architectural Styles & Sites:

  • Ancient & Temple Architecture:
    • Dravidian Style (South India): Brihadeeswarar Temple (Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu), Lepakshi Temple (Anantapur, Andhra Pradesh).
    • Nagara Style (North India): Konark Sun Temple (Odisha), Khajuraho Temples (Madhya Pradesh).
    • Buddhist Architecture: Sanchi Stupa (Madhya Pradesh), Amaravathi Stupa (Andhra Pradesh).
  • Islamic Architecture:
    • Mughal Architecture: Taj Mahal (Agra, Uttar Pradesh), Red Fort (Delhi), Charminar (Hyderabad, Telangana).
    • Sultanate Architecture: Qutub Minar (Delhi), Gol Gumbaz (Bijapur, Karnataka).
  • Colonial Architecture: Victoria Memorial (Kolkata), Gateway of India (Mumbai).
  • Modern Architecture: Lotus Temple (Delhi), Contemporary cityscapes (e.g., Bengaluru, Mumbai).
