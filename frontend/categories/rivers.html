Rivers of India

India is home to numerous rivers, which are vital lifelines, supporting agriculture, providing drinking water, generating power, and holding deep cultural and religious significance.

Major River Systems & Regions:

  • Himalayan Rivers:
    • Indus River System: Flows through Jammu & Kashmir.
    • Ganga River System: Originates in Uttarakhand, flows through Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, West Bengal. Major tributaries include Yamuna, Ghaghara, Kosi.
    • Brahmaputra River System: Flows through Arunachal Pradesh and Assam.
  • Peninsular Rivers:
    • East Flowing Rivers (into Bay of Bengal):
      • Godavari River: Originates in Maharashtra, flows through Telangana and Andhra Pradesh. "Dakshin Ganga."
      • Krishna River: Originates in Maharashtra, flows through Karnataka, Telangana, and Andhra Pradesh.
      • Mahanadi River: Flows through Chhattisgarh and Odisha.
      • Cauvery River: Flows through Karnataka and Tamil Nadu.
      • Penna River: Flows through Karnataka and Andhra Pradesh (Rayalaseema region).
    • West Flowing Rivers (into Arabian Sea):
      • Narmada River: Flows through Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Gujarat.
      • Tapi River: Flows through Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Gujarat.
